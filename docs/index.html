aimez builds evidence models and tools for decisions about complex physical systems. The work turns sparse observations, physical constraints and audit records into representations that can be inspected before they guide a route, forecast, paper or tool.

Company

The company works on the evidence gap between complex physical environments and the records available when people make decisions. Each project starts with a concrete representation problem, then publishes the artifact, demo or paper that exposes the boundary of the evidence.

friend, current product

friend is a city-scale evidence model of Manhattan, joining fragmented observations, camera-derived crowd pressure, a precomputed radiant-temperature layer and street-tree canopy into a block-scale representation of pedestrian conditions on the Manhattan walk graph.

Routing is the first application on top of that representation. Five cost encodings read the same evidence layers to compare shortest, crowd-avoiding, shade-seeking, comfort-aware and adaptive routes. Every narrated value is labeled live, partially live or deferred.
