aimez

aimez is the working home for a cross-domain research program using Manhattan as a model system for how stress patterns organize in a city.

Current work models high- and low-pressure areas on a pedestrian graph and asks how those patterns scale, how routing algorithms behave on top of them, and what that reveals about the interaction between human movement and urban form.

The broader program follows how local pressures produce organized behavior in systems where movement depends on fields rather than isolated parts.

Questions the work returns to include:

  • How do measured stress patterns scale across a graph?
  • How do routing algorithms behave when they move through those pressure fields?
  • What kinds of local pressure produce corridor-level response?
  • How can modeling make emergent organization observable?

The site stays intentionally spare. It points to working notes, demonstrated substrates, and packet-like research surfaces as they become stable enough to share.
