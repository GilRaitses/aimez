aimez

aimez is a research home for studying what happens when outputs that pass local tests meet scenes whose constraints those tests do not capture.

Current work uses pedestrian routing in Midtown Manhattan as one concrete case. A camera-derived stress field and a public-data capacity model show how a route can be locally valid under one criterion and structurally different under another.

The broader program follows the same problem across domains where artifacts are carried forward into later use.

Questions the work returns to include:

  • When does a route, score, or classification remain stable as its representation changes?
  • When do hidden constraints stay invisible until deployment or reuse?
  • What kinds of local changes produce non-local system response?
  • How can those shifts become inspectable before they turn into failure?

The site stays intentionally spare. It points to working notes, demonstrated substrates, and packet-like research surfaces as they become stable enough to share.
