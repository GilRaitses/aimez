Company

The company works on the evidence gap between complex physical environments and the records available when people make decisions. Each project starts with a concrete representation problem, then publishes the artifact, paper or public module that exposes the boundary of the evidence.

Klosr, current product

Beta / testflight · pilot-stage commercial workflow

Klosr is a Manhattan evidence model for pedestrian conditions. Sparse observations fuse onto a walk graph and validated evidence layers combine into a route objective. Coverage is the island walk graph under the published camera and model layers, not a global city claim.

Comfort-aware routing is one application on that representation, while five cost encodings read the same evidence layers to compare shortest, crowd-avoiding, shade-seeking, comfort-aware and adaptive routes. Each narrated value carries a live, partially live or deferred provenance label.

A pedestrian camera network that samples every 30 minutes builds a detailed record of street conditions, but that record is always of conditions 30 minutes ago. When the 08:00 cycle closes, it contains what the cameras observed from 07:30 to 08:00, while a decision that depends on conditions at 08:30 has nothing measured to consult. Field-JEPA is an experiment on forecasting the next crowding field from that measured cycle without waiting for the next cycle to close.

Application hosts remain in limited access while the public release prepares, so the public surface below is the Field-JEPA holdout timelapse. Research notes stay secondary while the release prepares. The pilot is designed for small campaign teams and independent storefront operators.

orcast

orcast is an evidence-gated encounter forecasting research platform for Southern Resident killer whales in the Salish Sea. A kernel intensity model with validation gates and provenance keeps displayed confidence tied to held-out skill, while the research workbench covers biologging-driven 3D reenactment and hydrophone views with a public launch planned for this summer.

Application hosts remain in limited access until that release, so the public module embedded below is the Orca Strike interaction surface.

Klosr · Field-JEPA Forecasting the crowding field

Beating persistence on Manhattan’s next half-hour of crowding, this holdout-day timelapse places continuous Field-JEPA forecasts beside observed crowding-sigma flowfields for the same cycle, with day of week and local time ticking between the panels and color encoding crowding sigma as a percentile rank of bias-corrected density inside a validated operating region.

On the full-island B cohort (339 cameras), Field-JEPA reaches 0.090 MAE at +30 minutes against a persistence bar of 0.105, a 14.2% reduction on a single held-out day (2026-06-29). The sigma field predicted here is a research result on Klosr’s crowding evidence layer, not a deployed routing feature.

Persistence, Field-JEPA and observed crowding sigma at +30 minutes appear as a continuous shoreline-clipped flowfield for a single holdout cycle, with persistence carrying the last observed field, Field-JEPA showing the forecast and the holdout panel showing what was observed.

Route overviews for two origin-destination pairs show the Village dog-run corridor and Midtown Grand Central to Carnegie Hall, with field-validated shade comparison scenes kept in the research pack as modeled shade and surface temperature on the street surface.

Research outputs

City evidence models

Manhattan is the pilot evidence model for pedestrian conditions on a walk graph. Camera-measured crowding cycles sit beside modeled shade and other evidence layers. Field-JEPA adds a predicted crowding layer beside the current-cycle measured field.

Physical-world evidence

orcast publishes gate-bounded encounter forecasting and claim-to-evidence binding for wildlife observation data, with Orca Strike as the current public interaction surface.
