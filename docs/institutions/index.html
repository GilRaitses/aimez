Institutional case study · July 2026

Designing Conditions at Vanderbilt

Vanderbilt has assembled an unusual concentration of computational research, clinical AI, music, design, public scholarship and cultural partnerships. The next institutional task is to give those assets recurring ways to form projects, share production environments and preserve what each collaboration learns.

Research question: Which institutional operating systems could Vanderbilt build during the next three years by connecting assets that already exist?

01 · Ranked opportunities

Seven structures that can begin from the present institution

The College of Connected Computing, VALIANT, Amplify, the Wond’ry, the CCA acquisition, Blair, VI4-AiR, VUMC, the Curb Center, McGee, Frist, NMAAM and the Innovation Neighborhood already supply the ingredients. Each proposal below adds a missing coordination mechanism rather than another independent institution.

Rank 01 · Low cost · Fast pilot

VALIANT × Blair Joint Production Fund

Three funded teams convert existing music-and-AI conversations into software, performances, datasets and installations.

Rank 02 · Proven model

AI-Arts Residency

VI4-AiR’s tested science-art residency structure moves into VALIANT and Connected Computing labs.

Rank 03 · Unique institutional asset

Nashville–San Francisco Fellowship

A named bi-coastal program turns the CCA acquisition into a working connection between Nashville research and Bay Area design.

Rank 04 · Public identity

Public AI Lab

Frist, NMAAM, VTNA, DSI, Curb and VALIANT co-commission public works built from cultural collections and research methods.

Rank 05 · External funding fit

Human-Centered AI Governance Center

Engineering, medicine, law, education and humanities build a shared research and policy-translation program.

Rank 06 · Long-term infrastructure

Innovation Neighborhood Studio

A shared production environment gives interdisciplinary work a physical home before district programming becomes fixed.

Rank 07 · Student pathway

Graduate Certificate

A formal credential joins AI, design and cultural production with advising and a public practicum.

02 · Shared architecture

An operating system for institutional convergence

Interdisciplinary work becomes repeatable when an institution can identify a problem, compose a mixed team, provide a shared production environment, require an inspectable output and carry the resulting methods into the next project. The proposals differ in scale, though each installs part of the same cycle.

Existing research and cultural assets
Required cross-domain team formation
Shared funding, space and production support
Public artifact and research output
Evaluation, memory and next project
The smallest credible intervention is a rule that changes who must work together and what they must make. A new building becomes useful only when those rules already exist.

03 · Initiative pages

Programs built from real pairings

01

VALIANT × Blair AI-Arts Joint Production Fund

Vanderbilt already has visible music-and-AI activity, yet the current bridges end with courses, symposia and conversation events. A joint fund would make co-production recurring and would judge the collaboration through the artifact it produces.

Assets already in place

VALIANT provides twelve labs and an institutional mandate for AI translation. Blair contributes the Music Cognition Lab, a Music Technology Minor and demonstrated faculty interest through its Music & AI symposium. Connected Computing, the Department of Art, McGee and the Heard Libraries Digital Lab extend the technical, visual and narrative range.

Missing mechanism

No standing program requires an AI researcher and a cultural producer to propose together, work through the same production process and deliver an artifact that can count in both fields. The present institution produces contact without a durable project-formation rule.

One-year pilot

Fund three twelve-month teams at $25,000 each. Every application must include a VALIANT or Connected Computing investigator, an arts or humanities investigator and a producer, curator or technical lead responsible for completion. Each team must release a performance, installation, dataset or piece of software within the award period.

Initial pairings

  • VALIANT audio researcher × Blair composer × Music Cognition Lab scientist
  • DSI data scientist × McGee narrative scholar × Department of Art faculty member
  • Amplify team × EADJ artist × Frist curator

Expected outputs

An interactive music system, a public Nashville music dataset, a narrative AI tool or documentary work, joint technical and artistic publications and a public presentation at Blair or Frist.

Risks and controls

The fund could dissolve into discussion or treat art as a demonstration surface for technical research. Artifact milestones, shared principal-investigator status and evaluation criteria written across both fields would keep production mutual.

Cost$75K, Year 1
LaunchSpring 2027
GovernanceVALIANT · Blair · CCC · A&S
PrecedentOpen Interval triadic teams
02

AI-Arts Residency: VI4-AiR into VALIANT and Connected Computing

VI4-AiR has already shown that Vanderbilt can pair artists and scientists through a bounded summer production cycle. Extending that governance model into AI research is the fastest path to a credible program with a Vanderbilt lineage.

Assets already in place

VI4-AiR has paired 119 artists with 71 scientists across more than one hundred collaborations and has documented the model through peer-reviewed evaluation. VALIANT, Amplify, the Curb Center, the University Museum of Art and the CCC AI Showcase provide hosts, public scholars and exhibition settings.

Missing mechanism

AI labs have no equivalent route for sustained artistic inquiry inside their research process. Occasional showcases reveal interest, though they begin after research choices have already been made.

One-year pilot

Select three artists for ten-week summer residencies in VALIANT labs. Pay each artist a $10,000 stipend, pair each with a VALIANT researcher and a Curb public scholar and reserve exhibition dates at Cohen Memorial Hall and the CCC AI Showcase before the residencies begin.

Initial pairings

  • Generative music artist × audio AI researcher
  • Data visualization artist × DSI scientist
  • Documentary filmmaker × McGee scholar × computer vision researcher

Expected outputs

Public installations, open creative tools, shared research documentation and an evaluation dataset that can support Mellon, NEH or NEA applications.

Risks and controls

A residency can become outreach that leaves the host lab unchanged. The program should document research decisions influenced by the resident, methods transferred in both directions and evidence of continued work after the exhibition.

Cost~$50K, Year 1
LaunchSummer 2027
GovernanceVI4-AiR template
PrecedentVI4-AiR · Eyebeam
03

Nashville–San Francisco Bi-Coastal AI-Design Fellowship

The CCA acquisition gives Vanderbilt a design and arts presence inside the Bay Area’s technology ecosystem. A named fellowship would make the Nashville–San Francisco relationship operational before the new campus settles into separate routines.

Assets already in place

The CCA campus and Wattis Institute bring design, architecture, fine arts and exhibition capacity. Nashville contributes VALIANT, Amplify, DSI, the Wond’ry, Blair and VUMC. Together they create a combination of clinical research, music, AI and design that no single campus presently contains.

Missing mechanism

No documented program makes a Nashville researcher and a San Francisco designer jointly responsible for one project. Without that mechanism, the two campuses can remain administratively connected while operating as separate intellectual environments.

One-year pilot

Begin with five virtual pairs during 2026–27, each receiving a small production budget and monthly critique. When the San Francisco campus opens, admit ten fellows who spend one semester in a Nashville host unit and one semester at the CCA Institute.

Initial pairings

  • VALIANT researcher × CCA computational artist
  • Wond’ry entrepreneur × CCA industrial designer
  • DSI scientist × CCA architect
  • Blair composer × Bay Area AI music technologist
  • VUMC engineer × CCA product designer

Expected outputs

Joint prototypes, Wattis exhibitions, open-source design tools, shared courses and public programs that connect Nashville’s cultural institutions with Bay Area partners.

Risks and controls

Opening delays or a narrow legacy-preservation mandate could postpone the connection. A program director appointed before campus launch can begin the virtual cohort and hold a budget specifically for cross-campus work.

Cost~$200K pilot
LaunchVirtual Fall 2026
GovernanceProvost-level co-directors
PrecedentAalto Design Factory
04

Public AI Lab: Nashville Cultural Institutions Partnership

Vanderbilt has strong cultural partners and substantial AI infrastructure, though those relationships rarely produce public computational works together. A co-commissioning lab would make Nashville’s collections, histories and communities part of the research environment.

Assets already in place

Frist has hosted an AI arts track, NMAAM has a $2 million Vanderbilt partnership, VTNA is applying AI to 62,000 hours of footage and the Curb Center, EADJ, Heard Libraries, DSI and VALIANT already span public scholarship, archives and computation.

Missing mechanism

Partnerships remain event-based or collection-specific. No unit regularly commissions a museum, archive or community partner together with an AI research team and carries the result through production, interpretation and public release.

One-year pilot

Co-commission one interactive exhibit with Frist and one African American music archive project with NMAAM. Locate the coordinating team in Heard Libraries or Curb, with joint technical leadership from DSI or Connected Computing.

Initial pairings

  • DSI scientists × NMAAM curators × Blair historians
  • VALIANT vision researchers × Frist educators
  • Heard Cloud Innovation Lab × music archive partners
  • EADJ artists × Vanderbilt University Museum of Art

Expected outputs

Interactive exhibits, open cultural datasets, archive interfaces, community-facing AI tools and a public residency for Nashville artists.

Risks and controls

Cultural partners could be treated as venues or data suppliers. Co-commission agreements should grant partners authority over research questions, interpretation, community access and the conditions under which collections enter AI workflows.

Cost~$150K, Year 1
LaunchSpring 2027
GovernanceCurb or Heard + DSI/CCC
PrecedentNEW INC
05

Human-Centered AI Governance Center

Vanderbilt’s AI governance capacity is distributed across clinical systems, HCI, law, education and humanities. A coordinated center would give those groups a shared research agenda and a route from institutional practice to public policy tools.

Assets already in place

STIR Lab studies socio-technical interaction, VUMC ADVANCE operates clinical AI governance, VAILL works on AI law, Peabody develops human-centered educational technology and the Robert Penn Warren Center, McGee and Vanderbilt Kennedy Center contribute humanistic, narrative and disability perspectives.

Missing mechanism

The groups have no common appointment structure, funding program or policy-translation process. Their work can remain adjacent even when the same system raises questions of clinical safety, consent, access, explanation and institutional accountability.

One-year pilot

Convene a four-school working group in Fall 2026, publish a joint research agenda, begin a public case seminar and prepare the next NEH Humanities Research Centers on Artificial Intelligence application. Design the first joint appointment that spans an AI unit and an arts, humanities or public-policy unit.

Initial pairings

  • STIR Lab × VUMC ADVANCE
  • VAILL × Peabody IDeaTE
  • McGee × Robert Penn Warren Center
  • Kennedy Center × clinical AI governance

Expected outputs

Joint empirical research, governance templates, policy briefs, public workshops, clinical case tools and a competitive federal center proposal.

Risks and controls

The center could become a seminar that comments on technical work after deployment. Its charter should include access to live institutional cases, shared methods, funded joint research and authority to develop operational governance artifacts.

CostUp to $500K / 36 months
LaunchWorking group Fall 2026
GovernanceEngineering · Medicine · Law · Peabody
Funding fitNEH · NIH
06

Innovation Neighborhood Shared AI-Arts Production Studio

Every recurring program in this packet will eventually need a place where computation, fabrication, performance and exhibition can occur within one production cycle. The Innovation Neighborhood planning period is the moment when that space can still be reserved.

Assets already in place

The forty-acre Innovation Neighborhood, the Wond’ry’s makerspaces, ACCRE, VALIANT, Blair performance facilities and the University Museum of Art collectively supply most capabilities. They remain distributed across spaces governed for different primary users.

Missing mechanism

No neutral production environment belongs to the collaboration itself. Teams must move between labs, studios and venues while negotiating access through the priorities of each host unit.

Phased pilot

Designate a temporary cross-school space inside the Wond’ry or Engineering during 2027. Use the pilot to measure demand, equipment use and project flow. Reserve 15,000–20,000 square feet in the Innovation Neighborhood for a permanent GPU-enabled studio, fabrication lab, recording environment and public demonstration space.

Initial pairings

  • VALIANT × Wond’ry computational fabrication
  • Blair × ACCRE generative performance
  • University Museum of Art × Connected Computing exhibitions
  • VUMC × CCA clinical design prototyping

Expected outputs

A shared creative-compute environment, a fabrication-to-performance pipeline, recurring public demonstrations and a permanent home for the fund, residencies and public lab.

Risks and controls

One school could capture the space or capital planning could close before governance is settled. The Provost should reserve the program area first and assign it to a cross-school council whose budget is independent of any single occupant.

Cost$5M–$10M capital
PilotTemporary space 2027
Permanent2029–30 target
PrecedentZKM Hertz Lab
07

Graduate Certificate in AI, Design and Cultural Production

Students can already assemble pieces of this education across Connected Computing, Peabody, Blair, the Wond’ry, Art and Curb. A certificate would turn personal navigation across those units into a recognized pathway with advising, production requirements and a cohort.

Assets already in place

The MS-AI and generative AI curriculum, Peabody IDeaTE, Blair’s Music Technology Minor, the Wond’ry’s Innovation and Design Strategy curriculum, Art’s creative coding courses and Curb’s Public Scholars Program already cover most of the academic terrain.

Missing mechanism

No credential recognizes work across these units, no advisor is responsible for the complete pathway and no practicum requires students to bring technical, design and cultural methods into one public project.

Program design

Create a fifteen-credit certificate housed administratively in Connected Computing and governed by a cross-school committee. Require Studio in AI, Human-Centered Design for AI, a new AI and Cultural Production course and a Public AI Practicum, with electives from Blair, Art, Law, McGee and the Wond’ry.

Student pathway

  • Coursework in at least three schools
  • Dedicated cross-school advisor
  • Participation in a funded production team or residency
  • Public practicum artifact with documented collaborators

Expected outputs

A recognizable talent pathway, stronger recruitment for the bi-coastal fellowship, public portfolio work and graduates who can move between research labs, cultural institutions and product teams.

Risks and controls

The certificate could become a list of electives without a shared intellectual experience. Cohort advising, a required practicum and participation in one of the packet’s production systems should define the credential.

Development~$100K
First cohortFall 2027
ScaleTuition-supported by Year 3
PrecedentCMU IDeATe

04 · Implementation sequence

Begin with rules, prove the production cycle, then build the space

Phase 1 · 2026–27

Form and test

  • Convene a lightweight Cross-School AI-Arts Council.
  • Launch the three-team Joint Production Fund.
  • Run the first VALIANT artist residencies.
  • Name the Nashville–SF program director.
  • Submit the human-centered AI center proposal.
Phase 2 · 2027–28

Connect and credential

  • Open the residential bi-coastal fellowship.
  • Launch the graduate certificate.
  • Co-commission Frist and NMAAM public AI projects.
  • Exhibit residency and fund outputs together.
  • Operate a temporary shared production studio.
Phase 3 · 2028–30

Institutionalize and build

  • Use evaluation evidence to secure sustained external funding.
  • Create joint appointments around proven collaborations.
  • Embed the permanent studio in the Innovation Neighborhood.
  • Connect Nashville, San Francisco and public partners through recurring cohorts.
