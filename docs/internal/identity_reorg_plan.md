# Identity Reorganization Plan

## Purpose

This memo names the first internal reorganization of `aimez` after the IST 675 demonstration and the emergence of two new theory anchors: Sane et al. on Sharpness Dimension / Edge of Stability, and Sridhar et al. on Allocentric Flocking. The reorganization is strategic first. No public-site edits are implied by this memo.

The goal is to move `aimez` from a site that reads primarily as a diagnostic initiative around coherence in complex information environments toward a clearer statement of what the program now is: a cross-domain applied research home investigating how artifacts, representations, and decisions remain usable through time and evaluable across contexts when systems operate on fields of constraint that exceed what interfaces make visible.

## What stays

The current core should remain intact.

1. `aimez` remains artifact-centered rather than model-centered.
2. The primary concern remains applicability under reuse rather than correctness at generation time.
3. The site still distinguishes applied research, theory, and infrastructure as different layers of the same structural problem.
4. The public posture should remain modest. The site should continue to foreground diagnostic gaps and hidden dependencies rather than claims of superiority, novelty, or broad capability.

These constraints come from [`docs/internal/internal_rule.md`](/Users/gilraitses/aimez/docs/internal/internal_rule.md) and should not be weakened.

## What changes

The current public copy understates the program in three ways.

1. It frames `aimez` almost entirely as a diagnostic initiative. That is true but incomplete now that there is a demonstrated applied substrate in the mobility work.
2. It does not name the temporal or field-level commitments that now clearly anchor the work. The demonstrated substrate depends on scene-level, attractor-level, and second-order structure that pointwise descriptions do not capture.
3. It gives no place for actual artifacts to appear, even privately, so readers cannot see the work that now grounds the program's claims.

The reorganization should therefore cultivate a more exact center of gravity:

- structure-aware intelligence research
- cross-domain convergence around representational geometry, temporal persistence, and applicability under reuse
- field-level or second-order structure rather than pointwise output success
- discoverative intelligence as a value orientation, not yet a public slogan
- structured temporal intelligence as a useful external language for later phases, not the homepage headline

## The new posture in one sentence

This sentence is not for immediate homepage use. It is the internal target the site should grow toward:

> `aimez` is a cross-domain applied research platform for structure-aware intelligence. It investigates how artifacts, representations, and decisions persist through time, shift across contexts, and remain evaluable under reuse when systems operate on fields of constraint that exceed what interfaces make visible.

## Why this shift is warranted now

Three new materials justify the shift.

1. The IST 675 demonstration package turned the abstract structural claim into a measurable applied substrate. It now exists as a self-contained appendix and slide deck inside `IST675/ist_675_final_proj_core/demonstration/`.
2. The post-presentation theory note on Sharpness Dimension made the local-versus-attractor distinction explicit as a formal anchor for the structural problem. See [`IST675/ist_675_final_proj_core/post_presentation_followup/03_formal_theory_integration_sharpness_dimension.md`](/Users/gilraitses/IST675/ist_675_final_proj_core/post_presentation_followup/03_formal_theory_integration_sharpness_dimension.md).
3. The Sridhar et al. Allocentric Flocking paper supplied a strong biological precedent for why world-anchored or scene-anchored representations matter for coherent system-level response.

These are not decorative references. Together they make the current `aimez` language too narrow.

## Do / don't list for future copy

### Do

- keep the writing artifact-centered
- talk about applicability, evaluability, and durability under reuse
- name the field-level or second-order structure where it clarifies the problem
- describe demonstrated substrates as evidence that the program can instantiate its claims
- connect cross-domain materials by the structural problem they share, not by superficial theme

### Don't

- do not make `aimez` sound like a deep learning theory lab
- do not make `aimez` sound like an active-matter or flocking lab
- do not make `aimez` sound like a general AI manifesto
- do not import the Sane or Sridhar vocabulary wholesale into public-facing copy
- do not foreground discoverative intelligence or structured temporal intelligence as public branding until phase 2 or 3
- do not let the site imply more public maturity than the materials support

## Phase-1 outcome

At the end of phase 1, the public homepage may still be unchanged. Success is internal:

1. the program's identity is named more exactly
2. the new theory anchors are mapped to the old language without drift
3. the Jen-facing packet has a private home in the `aimez` architecture
4. the future site changes have a cleaner target and less noise

This is the right first step because it stabilizes the writing before the site starts carrying more public material.
