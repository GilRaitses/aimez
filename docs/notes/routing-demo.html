Routing demo: stress-aware pedestrian routing

Pick an origin and a destination in the Midtown corridor, then compare a shortest route against a stress-aware route. The router scores every block on measured camera stress, not just distance, and the figure and metrics are computed live on the pax routing engine.

Origin and destination are landmarks inside the Midtown corridor (streets 42 to 59, avenues 3 to 9). The first request after a quiet period takes a few seconds to warm up.

How it works

The camera pipeline reads public traffic-camera frames and turns them into a per-block stress value from 0 to 1. The router runs A* search on the Midtown corridor graph with an edge cost of 1 + alpha * stress, so a calmer block costs less to cross. Each request snaps the chosen landmarks to the nearest intersection, computes both routes and renders the comparison.

This is the public research demonstration of the mobility substrate. Some broader diagnostic-infrastructure concepts in the research program are the subject of a filed U.S. provisional patent application. Built and orchestrated with Central Casting.
