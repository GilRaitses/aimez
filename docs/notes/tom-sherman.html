A note for Tom

Time-based media, modeled environments, and emergent structure

I put this page together because the research program I have been building returns to questions I first learned to take seriously through time-based media: how a situation unfolds, how attention changes across time, and how an environment becomes legible only through the structure of its representation.

The project in one concrete case

The current applied case is a pedestrian-routing substrate for Midtown Manhattan. A standard route planner gives the fastest route. My version also reads a second-order field: camera-derived stress, sidewalk width, scaffolding, frontage load, and station envelopes. Once that field is represented, the route changes and load redistributes across the corridor.

Why this made me think of time-based media

The route result is only the entry point. The larger question is the relation between a local output and the scene it enters. The route is an artifact. The city corridor is a time-based environment. The model becomes a way of representing the field that the artifact will later answer to. That is where the problem meets media theory: the work is about how an environment is made perceptible through a representational system.

Why the flocking bridge helps

The short video segment below gives a compact version of the bridge I am trying to understand. It moves from rule-based flocking models to navigation models built from how animals encode space, and then back out to crowds and active matter. The part I care about is the shift from local, agent-centered information to world-anchored representation.

The 2025 flocking paper linked from that discussion works as a related anchor because it shows how coherent collective behavior can depend on the reference frame through which a system encodes the world. The city, the flock, the crowd, and the mediated image are different objects. The shared problem is how representation and environment shape what a system can do over time.

The deeper theoretical direction

A second nearby idea comes from work on generalization at the edge of stability: a system's behavior may be governed less by one local output and more by the lower-dimensional structure its dynamics actually explore. For my work, that helps name the question: the relevant object may not be one best route, but the shape of the route space once the scene's constraints are represented.

What I would value from you

I would value your read on the media-theory side of this: whether the environment-as-media frame is real here, whether the project is making structure perceptible in a way that connects to time-based media, and where the language can become more precise or less technical.
