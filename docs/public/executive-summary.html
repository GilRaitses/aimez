Measured fields on pedestrian graphs

aimez · camera stress on a Midtown walking graph built from NYC open data

The program locks a corridor graph, attaches a DOT camera stress field, then compares routes on measured exposure. The public demo uses one origin–destination pair: Grand Central to Carnegie Hall.

At equal hop count, the stress-aware path carries lower accumulated camera stress than the shortest-hop path. That pair is the worked case study; routing is one export from a wider measured-city program.

Problem

Map distance and walking burden diverge

Apps optimize hops or minutes. Walkers meet crowding, sheds, station egress and uneven capacity. Local pressure sits on the graph where standard routers use hop count alone.

The program represents that pressure on a graph and compares routes on measured exposure from the stress field.

Pilot

Midtown corridor

40th–59th St, Lexington to 8th Ave · ~300 nodes · decision-locked stress field · multi-layer capacity from NYC Open Data.

Same graph, varying fields. A bounded surface partners can evaluate at corridor scale.

open data → stress field → routing demo → validation

Methods

Three streams → one substrate

  1. Open civic data. Supply-side capacity on cross-street edges.
  2. Public camera sensing. Demand-side stress on the graph.
  3. Field calibration. Lived-knowledge audit on reference blocks.

Stress topology is read before routing is switched on.
