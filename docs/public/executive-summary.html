aimez · more ways to like the walk

Outcome: how we engage the city between destinations. Shared stress on a public graph, hop-equivalent routes, and room to meet, gather and move the way people prefer.

Case study using a vision AI system to reconstruct stress-field topology from DOT camera data and NYC open data on a Manhattan pedestrian graph.

Grand Central → Carnegie Hall: equal hop count; lower accumulated camera stress on the stress-aware path.

Public-good case

What this demonstration claims

Public inputs, a locked Manhattan corridor graph, a camera stress field computed before routing, and route pairs compared on measured exposure at equal hop count.

  • Inputs: NYC Open Data tables plus DOT traffic cameras (inventory on the next slide).
  • Audit point: stress topology is fixed before any path algorithm runs.
  • Demo result: Grand Central → Carnegie Hall, same hops, lower accumulated camera stress on the stress-aware path.
  • Scope: one corridor (~300 nodes), reproducible snapshot, corridor-scale claims only.
  • Commercial boundary: fusion, calibration and routing cost function stay proprietary; open tables stay open.

Problem

Map distance and walking burden diverge

Apps optimize hops or minutes. Walkers meet crowding, sheds, station egress and uneven capacity. Local pressure sits on the graph where standard routers use hop count alone.

The program represents that pressure on a graph and compares routes on measured exposure from the stress field.

Pilot

Manhattan corridor (40th–59th St)

Lexington to 8th Ave · ~300 nodes · decision-locked stress field · multi-layer capacity from NYC Open Data.

Same graph, varying fields. A bounded surface partners can evaluate at corridor scale.

open data → stress field → routing demo → validation

Methods

Three streams → one substrate

  1. Open civic data. Supply-side capacity on cross-street edges.
  2. Public camera sensing. Demand-side stress on the graph.
  3. Field calibration. Lived-knowledge audit on reference blocks.

Stress topology is read before routing is switched on.
