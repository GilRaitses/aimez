Public · Representation pipeline

From camera frames to graph stress

This is category A in the executive summary map for how sensing becomes a field the router can read. It matches the IST 675 demonstration narrative at a high level; the full course appendix HTML lives outside this repo unless synced to AWS (see manifest).

Stages

  1. Ingest. Public NYC traffic camera frames on a fixed cadence (corridor-scale camera manifest).
  2. Perceive. YOLO (pedestrians/vehicles), CLIP scene semantics, Detectron2 density; fused into a per-camera stress score in [0, 1].
  3. Spatialize. Voronoi tessellation from camera positions; optional IDW smoothing between cells.
  4. Graph bind. Stress assigned to corridor intersections and directed edges; combined with Open Data capacity layers for routing cost.
  5. Route. Shortest vs crowd-avoiding (live routing demo).

What is not on this page

Supply-side capacity (sidewalk width, sheds, frontage, stations) comes from NYC Open Data, not from cameras. Orthogonality of the two layers is shown in the canonical walkthrough (§4.1). Substrate and validation figures are in Canonical figures walkthrough.
